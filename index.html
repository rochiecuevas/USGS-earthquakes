Today's Earthquakes

An earthquake is a sudden shaking of the ground caused by sudden movement on a faultline. The U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) Earthquakes Hazards Programme provides real-time data about earthquakes. Today's Earthquakes collects data from USGS (which is updated every five minutes) and maps them to visualise the locations of these earthquakes and inform the public about their magnitudes, significance, and depth.

Each circle corresponds to an earthquake. The size of the circle and its colour indicates its magnitude. An earthquake's magnitude, or the measure of the energy released during an earthquake, ranges from 1 to more than 9. Use the legend at the bottom right to see the colours corresponding to different earthquake magnitudes. Also, click on the different circles to find more information about them.

To see the earthquakes in satellite view or in street map view, just choose the map view type from the options in the upper right hand box.
